ACL Tear Awareness

Understand the risk, signs, and prevention methods for ACL injuries. Awareness is key to protecting your knees.

Risk Factors & Causes

  • Sudden deceleration or pivoting movements in sports
  • Poor landing technique or imbalance
  • Previous knee injuries or muscular weakness
  • Gender-specific risk: higher in female athletes

Signs & Prevention

  • Pop sound or sudden pain during activity
  • Immediate swelling or instability
  • Strengthening & dynamic stability exercises reduce risk
  • Education on proper landing and cutting techniques

Warmup & Strength Routine

Prevent ACL injuries by performing these warmups and strengthening exercises.

Dynamic Warmup

  1. Leg swings — front-to-back and side-to-side: 10 each leg
  2. Walking lunges with a twist: 10 each side
  3. Glute bridges: 10–15 reps
  4. Light jogging or skipping: 60 seconds
  5. High knees or agility ladder drills: 30–45 seconds

Strength & Control

  1. Single-leg Romanian deadlift — 8–12 reps per leg
  2. Bulgarian split squat — 8–10 reps per leg
  3. Side-lying clamshells — 12–20 reps per side
  4. Nordic hamstring curls — 6–10 reps
  5. Box drop landings — 5–8 reps, focus on knee alignment
